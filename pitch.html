Fathom

A protocol to create and assess meaningful credentials through the consensus of knowledge communities

The Problem

Granular understanding of abilities and skills enables more optimal organization but...


An insitutional trust model for credentials cannot scale to provide these credentials

Why?

In short, social scalabilty and Campbell's Law

The Solution

A decentralized network based system of credentials

Project History

  • P2P Learning and Education
  • Key-graph style reputation system
  • Protocols and network solutions

How does it Work

Communities are built around credentials

  • They can define any arbitrary rules, definitions, or meanings
  • They are a subset of parent communities
  • They have children communities
↓

Users pay tokens to communities to get assessed

  • They pay per assessor

↓

Pool of assessors are called

  • Assessors can accept by staking tokens

↓

The assessment process

  • Each assessor individually assesses
  • They commit-reveal their scores
  • Majority rewarded, minority punished

Outcomes

  • Assessee joins community if they pass
  • Assessment contributes to community definition

Advantages

  • Centripetal Standards and procedures
  • Scalable trust paradigm

Products and Services

Decentralized Job Markets

Credential Consumers

The field

  • Ethlance, CommitEth, OpenCollectives; Crypto and Open Source newcomers

Business Model

We solve expensive contracting by providing a decentralized platform to help individuals cheaply hire contractors.

We make money by charging individuals for extra trust and security (hold funds, Crypto to Fiat, etc)

Hiring

Credential consumers

Credentials and the assessment procedure can replace early stages in the hiring process

Especially valuable in field defining companies

Ecosystem Benefits

Business Model

We solve expensive and ineffective early hiring by providing a secure credential system to help employers initially screen applications

Online Social Learning

Credential Producers (and consumers)
