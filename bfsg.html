So why blockchains?

They're a paradigm for socially scalable rules!

The first approach:

Blockchain anchored credentials

A standard format for

  • Defining a credential
  • Who issued it
  • Who it is issued to
  • Other rules around it

How it works

  • Credentials are related to cryptographic identities
  • Are hashed and stored on-chain

Why it's important

  • Self-Sovereign Identity
  • Long-term security
  • Forms a foundation

Who?

  • BlockCerts
  • uPort

What this doesn't address

Credentials still come from central issuers

AND

One can trust their integrity not their validity

This leads back to centralized trust

Luckily, validity is a social process

The second approach

Blockchain generated credentials

A protocol

  • Leveraging communities to create standards and concepts
  • incentivizing the inputs of knowledgeable peers
  • allowing anyone to get assessed or create new credentials

The process

When an individual wants a credential:

  1. They pay tokens into the system
  2. Assessors are drawn from the community around the credential and stake
  3. Assessors assess individually
  4. Assessors are rewarded in relation to each other

Why this is cool

  • This is how people build valid credentials naturally
  • It is really abstract
  • Anyone can trust in it
This creates is a universal credential ecosystem, where individuals are free to learn what they please how they please and earn credentials that enable them to directly and meaningfully interact with anyone in the world.
